# EF Agent Co‑Pilot 

## What is EF Agent Co‑Pilot? 

EF Agent Co‑Pilot is your AI assistant inside the EFCX Agent Desktop.  
It helps your agents respond to customers faster and more accurately, with less effort, by using your own knowledge base and AI models.

EF Agent Co‑Pilot:

  * Understands customer conversations in **real time** , for both **chat and voice (via call transcription)**.

  * Suggests **answers and next steps** to agents while they work.

  * Looks into your **knowledge base and documents** to find the right information.

  * Creates **short summaries** of conversations.

  * Detects **customer sentiment** at the end of the conversation.




This guide focuses on **what you get in the current release** , from an end‑user (agent/customer) point of view.

## How does EF Agent Co‑Pilot work?

EF Agent Co‑Pilot runs alongside the agent inside EFCX Agent Desk:

  1. It **reads the ongoing conversation**

     * For **chat** , it uses the live text.

     * For **voice** , it uses the **call transcript** generated from the audio.

  2. It uses AI (NLU + LLM) to understand:

     * What the customer is talking about (topic / intent).

     * What information is needed to solve the issue.

  3. It searches your **KB Server** :

     * PDFs you uploaded.

     * Web pages / URLs you connected.

     * Other indexed content.

  4. It then:

     * Suggests **short answers** and **drafted replies**.

     * Shows **relevant knowledge links**.

     * Generates **summaries** and **post‑conversation sentiment**.




Agents stay in full control: they choose which suggestions to use, edit, or ignore.

## Key features 

### Smart agent assistance during conversations

EF Agent Co‑Pilot provides real‑time assistance for agents on **both chat and voice interactions** :

  * For **chat** , it reacts to each new customer message.

  * For **voice calls** , it reacts to the **live or recent transcript** of what was said on the call.




Agents see suggestions in a **Co‑Pilot panel** next to the conversation and can insert or adapt them as needed.

#### NLU‑based understanding & suggestions

**What it is**

The system uses NLU (Natural Language Understanding) to analyze what the customer is saying in:

  * **Chat messages** , and

  * **Voice calls (via call transcription)**.




It detects:

  * The **topic** (for example: “password reset”, “invoice question”, “delivery delay”).

  * The **intent** (what the customer wants to achieve).




Based on this, Co‑Pilot shows:

  * Suggested **short replies** the agent can send.

  * Suggested **articles or snippets** from the knowledge base.

  * Suggested **next questions** to clarify the issue.




**What the agent sees**

In the Co‑Pilot panel, which updates as the conversation continues (chat or voice transcript):

  * 2–3 possible replies.

  * 2–3 relevant KB articles with a short description.




The agent can:

  * Click a reply suggestion to insert it into the reply box and edit before sending.

  * Click a KB suggestion to open the article or snippet for more details.




This works the same way whether the conversation started as **chat** or a **voice call** (through its transcript).

#### LLM‑based drafted replies

**What it is**

When agents need a complete, well‑worded answer, they can ask Co‑Pilot to **draft a full reply**.

The AI uses:

  * The **conversation history** (chat or voice transcript).

  * Relevant **knowledge base content** retrieved from the KB Server via Agentic RAG.

  * The organization’s preferred **tone and language** (for example: professional, friendly).




**How it works for the agent**

  1. The agent clicks a button such as **“Generate with Co‑Pilot”** near the reply box.

  2. Co‑Pilot:

     * Reads the most recent customer and agent messages (or the latest part of the call transcript).

     * Fetches relevant information from the KB Server.

     * Proposes 1–3 drafted replies.

  3. The agent can:

     * Use a reply as‑is.

     * Edit it before sending.

     * Discard it and write their own.




This helps agents respond **faster** , more **consistently** , and in line with **your policies and knowledge**.

### AI that uses your own knowledge (Agentic RAG)

EF Agent Co‑Pilot uses an **Agentic Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG)** approach.

In simple terms: the AI **does not answer from generic memory alone**. It actively searches **your own knowledge base** first, then composes a response based on what it finds.

#### What this means for you

When a customer asks something (in chat or via a voice call that is transcribed):

  1. Co‑Pilot reads the latest part of the conversation.

  2. It searches the **KB Server** for the most relevant:

     * Articles

     * PDF sections

     * Web pages (scraped content)

  3. The AI can **make several search calls** , refining what it needs.

  4. It then uses those results to:

     * Build suggested answers.

     * Provide step‑by‑step procedures.

     * Show supporting KB links.




#### What the agent sees

  * Suggested replies that include a **“Source: …”** reference with:

    * Article name

    * Short snippet

    * A clickable link

  * A small list of **“Related Knowledge”** on the side, which can be opened in a new tab or side panel.




This ensures your answers are:

  * **Consistent** – all agents rely on the same sources.

  * **Up‑to‑date** – responses are based on your current KB content.

  * **Traceable** – agents can always see where an answer came from.




### Conversation summaries & sentiment

EF Agent Co‑Pilot creates short summaries and detects final customer sentiment at key points in the conversation. This applies to both **chat** and **voice** once the call has a transcript.

#### Where summaries are created

  1. **When a bot transfers to a human agent (Virtual Agent Summary)**

     * As soon as a virtual agent (bot) hands over the conversation:

       * Co‑Pilot generates a **short summary** of what happened with the bot.

     * When the human agent accepts:

       * They see a few lines describing:

         * Why the customer contacted you.

         * Which questions were asked.

         * Any steps the bot already took.

  2. **When one agent transfers to another agent (Transfer Summary)**

     * When Agent A transfers to Agent B:

       * Co‑Pilot creates a **summary of the conversation so far** (for chat or call transcript).

     * Only Agent B (the receiving agent) sees this summary.

     * This helps the new agent get up to speed quickly.

  3. **When the conversation ends (Final Summary)**

     * When the last participant leaves and the conversation is closed:

       * Co‑Pilot creates a **final summary** of the entire interaction.

     * This is saved with the conversation record and can be:

       * Viewed later by QA and supervisors.

       * Used for reporting and analytics.




All summaries are stored in the **Conversation Data** section inside Agent Desk.

#### Post‑conversation sentiment

In the current MVP:

  * **Only post‑conversation sentiment** is supported (no real‑time sentiment yet).




**What it does**

After the conversation ends (chat or transcribed voice call), Co‑Pilot:

  * Analyzes the full interaction.

  * Assigns an overall **sentiment** , such as:

    * Positive

    * Negative

    * Neutral

  * Optionally adds emotion / confidence scores.




**What this is used for**

  * Tracking the **quality of interactions**.

  * Identifying:

    * Upset or unhappy customers.

    * Agents or topics with recurring negative sentiment.

  * Feeding data into:

    * Reports.

    * Coaching and QA processes.

    * Trend analysis.




This becomes your **official post‑conversation sentiment** stored with each interaction.

### Knowledge Base (KB) Server – your content hub

The **KB Server** is the engine that connects your internal content to EF Agent Co‑Pilot.

#### What you can do with the KB Server

  1. **Upload documents**

     * Upload **PDF files** (and other supported formats).

     * The system automatically:

       * Extracts the text.

       * Splits it into small, meaningful sections.

       * Creates **embeddings** (vector representations) to enable smart search.

  2. **Add web content (URL scraping)**

     * Provide any **URL or website** you want to use as a knowledge source.

     * The KB Server:

       * Scrapes the page.

       * Extracts the text.

       * Keeps an index of that content.

     * This is useful for:

       * Online help centers.

       * Product documentation sites.

       * Internal wiki pages that are accessible.

  3. **Semantic search APIs**

     * The KB Server offers APIs for **semantic search**.

     * EF Agent Co‑Pilot and the LLM connector call these APIs to:

       * Find the most relevant snippets for a given question.

       * Bring back title, snippet, and link.

  4. **Keep content up to date**

     * When documents change or web pages are updated:

       * Admins can re‑index or schedule regular crawls.

     * This ensures the AI always uses **fresh information**.




#### What this means for your team

  * You remain in **full control** of what the AI knows.

  * You can start with:

    * A small set of PDFs.

    * A few important URLs.

  * Then gradually expand to more content over time as your needs grow.

## What’s planned for future releases (not available yet)

The following are **planned / next‑release** capabilities. They are **not available in the current scope** , but are part of the EF Agent Co‑Pilot roadmap:

  * **Real‑time customer sentiment** (during the conversation, not only after it ends).

  * **Agent notes with AI help** (AI‑drafted internal notes).

  * **Wrap‑up suggestions / auto wrap‑up improvements** (AI recommending disposition codes).

  * **Real‑time trends and topic clustering** (spotting emerging issues).

  * **CSAT prediction** from conversation text.

  * **Advanced “Next Best Actions”** and deeper agent guidance flows.

  * **Broader channel coverage** (for example, email drafting with feature parity).

  * **Feedback on AI suggestions** (thumbs up/down, learning from agent feedback).




These future features will build on the same core components used today:  
**EFCX Agent Desk, Conversation Studio, AI Analyzer, LLM Connector, and KB Server.**
