# Wrap-up and Follow-up Work

As an Agent (Amy), properly concluding your conversations is vital for accurate reporting and future customer interactions. This guide covers how to add **Wrap-up Codes** and **Notes** to summarize the outcome of your interactions.

## 1. Concluding a Conversation
When you finish a conversation, the system often requires you to provide a summary of the interaction.

### Using Wrap-up Codes:
Click the **Notes** icon on your control toolbar to open the wrap-up window.
- **Select a Category:** Choose the broad topic of the interaction (e.g., Billing, Support).
- **Select a Reason:** Choose the specific outcome (e.g., Refund Issued, Issue Escalated).
- **Multiple Codes:** You can apply up to **five wrap-up codes** per activity.

## 2. Adding Detailed Notes
After selecting your wrap-up codes, type a brief, professional summary in the **Notes** field.
- **Purpose:** These notes appear in the customer's history, helping the next agent understand exactly what was resolved.
- **Visibility:** Notes are internal and will not be visible to the customer.

## 3. The Wrap-up Timer
For high-volume channels, your administrator might enable a **Wrap-up Timer**.
- **The Countdown:** You will see a timer on the screen (usually 60 seconds) to complete your wrap-up work.
- **The Workflow:** If you leave the conversation before the timer expires, the interaction is officially closed. If the timer expires without a wrap-up, the interaction is marked as "Closed without Wrap-up."

### Pro-Tips for Amy:
1. **Consistency:** Always use the same wrap-up codes for the same types of issues—this ensures your supervisor (Sam) can accurately track team performance.
2. **Post-Call Work (Voice):** For voice calls, your status changes to **Wrap-up** automatically, preventing new calls from arriving until you finish your notes and set yourself to **Ready**.
