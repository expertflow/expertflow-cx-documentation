# AI-Driven Quality Management

Traditional quality assurance relies on manual evaluation of a small sample of agent interactions. This approach is resource-intensive, inconsistent across evaluators, and provides delayed feedback. The ExpertFlow AI-Driven Quality Management module addresses these limitations by enabling automated, objective evaluation at scale.

## The Problem with Manual QA

- Manual evaluation can only cover a **small percentage** of all interactions.
- Scoring varies between evaluators, introducing **inconsistency**.
- Feedback is delivered after the fact — agents wait days or weeks for QA results.
- High-volume contact centers cannot scale manual QA without significantly increasing headcount.

## How AI-Driven QM Works

The AI engine processes interaction data — transcripts, metadata, and conversation context — and automatically scores each interaction based on the configured evaluation form. This produces consistent, objective results across 100% of interactions.

### Two Operational Modes

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| **Full Automation** | The AI evaluates 100% of closed interactions. No human evaluator involvement required. |
| **Hybrid Mode** | A configurable percentage of interactions is assigned to AI; the remainder goes to human evaluators. Enables human oversight for complex or flagged interactions. |

## Key Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---|---|
| **Full coverage** | Every interaction is scored, not just a random sample. |
| **Consistency** | AI applies the same evaluation criteria uniformly across all interactions. |
| **Reduced manual workload** | Quality Managers spend less time on routine scoring and more on coaching and exception handling. |
| **Faster feedback** | Scores are available immediately after a conversation closes. |

## Supported Languages

The AI evaluation engine currently supports:
- **English**
- **Arabic** (Egyptian dialect)

## Integration with the QM Workflow

AI-generated evaluations appear in the same QM interface as human evaluations. Quality Managers can:
- Review AI scores and override them if needed.
- Compare AI scores with human evaluator scores for calibration.
- Use AI results to identify coaching opportunities and systemic issues.
