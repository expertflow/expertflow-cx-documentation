# Designing Evaluation Forms

As a Quality Manager (Quentin), you define the standards for "Good Service" through **Evaluation Forms** (Questionnaires). These forms allow your Evaluators (Eva) to provide objective, mathematical scores for every interaction.

## 1. Defining Your Rubric
A professional evaluation form is organized into logical sections that reflect your business priorities.
- **Communication:** Tone, clarity, and empathy.
- **Compliance:** Script adherence and verification steps.
- **Problem Solving:** Accuracy of information and resolution speed.

## 2. Setting Up Weightage
Weighting ensures that critical mistakes (like failing a security check) impact the score more than minor ones (like a slow greeting).
1.  **Form Weight:** Every form must sum to **100%**. 
2.  **Section Weight:** Assign a percentage to each section (e.g., Compliance = 40%, Communication = 30%).
3.  **Question Weight:** Inside each section, assign percentages to individual questions. 

## 3. Question Types
You can choose how Eva provides her feedback:
- **Weighted Single Select:** The only type that impacts the mathematical score (e.g., "Yes/No", "1-5 Scale").
- **Non-weighted Dropdowns:** Useful for categorizing issues without affecting the score.
- **Text Inputs:** Mandatory for providing qualitative feedback or coaching notes.

## 4. Validating the Form
Before you can use a form in a [Review Schedule](Quality-Assurance-Reports.md), the system must verify the math.
- **Automatic Check:** The Form Builder will highlight any section where the questions do not sum to 100%. 
- **Publishing:** Once validated, click **Save** to make the form available for your evaluation team.

### Best Practices for Quentin:
- **Calibration:** Periodically meet with your evaluators to ensure they are interpreting the rubric criteria identically.
- **Simplicity:** Don't overload Eva with too many questions. A focused rubric leads to more accurate and faster audits.
