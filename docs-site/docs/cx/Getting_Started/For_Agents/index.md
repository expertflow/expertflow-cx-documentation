# The Agent's Quick-Start Guide

Welcome to ExpertFlow CX! This guide will walk you through your first interaction, from logging in to wrapping up a conversation.

## Step 1: Getting In
1. Navigate to your company's **Agent Desk** URL.
2. Enter your credentials provided by your administrator.
3. **Cisco Users:** If you are using Cisco Finesse, you will be logged into ExpertFlow automatically when you sign into Finesse.

## Step 2: Set Your Status
Before you can receive customers, you must tell the system you are ready.
1. Click the **Status** icon in the top toolbar.
2. Select **Ready** to start receiving chats and calls.
3. If you need a break, select **Not Ready** and choose a reason (e.g., "Lunch" or "Meeting").

## Step 3: Handling Your First Interaction

### Accepting the Request
When a customer contacts you, a notification will appear in your browser and on the Agent Desk.
- Click **Accept** on the incoming alert.
- The **Conversation View** will open automatically.

> **Pull-based queues:** Some queues require you to pull work manually rather than wait for an alert. If you are not receiving incoming requests, ask your administrator whether your queue is push or pull-based.

### What You Can See
The Conversation View shows everything you need to handle the interaction:
- **Customer details** — name, contact history, and profile on the right panel
- **Channel** — the channel the customer is contacting you on (chat, voice, WhatsApp, etc.)
- **Conversation history** — previous interactions with this customer

### Communicating with the Customer
- **Plain Text:** Type your message in the composer at the bottom.
- **Pause/Resume:** Use the **Pause** button to temporarily hold the conversation if you need time to look something up. Click **Resume** when you are ready to continue.
- **Whisper (Internal):** Need help? Click the **Yellow Whisper Tab** to chat with your supervisor. The customer **cannot** see these messages.
- **Collaborating:** Use the **Transfer** or **Conference** buttons to bring in another agent if needed.

> **Need to reach a customer first?** See [Making an Outbound Contact](../../How-to_Guides/Agent/Making-an-Outbound-Contact.md) for how to initiate voice calls, WhatsApp messages, and SMS.

## Step 4: Resolving & Wrapping Up
Once the customer's issue is solved, you must close the interaction properly.
1. Click the **Leave** or **End** button.
2. The **Wrap-up Screen** will appear.
3. Select a **Category** and **Reason** for the conversation (e.g., "Support > Billing").
4. Add any internal notes that might help the next agent.
5. Assign a **Label** to tag the conversation (e.g., "Complaint", "Escalation") if relevant.
6. Click **Submit** to return to a "Ready" state.

## Step 5: Other Things to Know

### Notifications & Alerts
Your browser must have sound and notifications enabled for you to receive alerts when a new conversation arrives.
- A **banner notification** appears on screen when a new request comes in.
- A **sound beep** plays on every new incoming message. This cannot be disabled.
- If you are missing requests, check that your browser has permission to send notifications. See [Browser and Sound Notifications](../../Capabilities/Digital_Channels/Browser-and-Sound-Notifications.md) for setup guidance.

### Voice & Calls
If your role involves handling phone calls:
- The **CTI toolbar** appears during voice conversations with controls for mute/unmute, hold/resume, and end call. See the [CTI Call Controls guide](../../Capabilities/Voice_and_Video/CTI-Call-Controls.md) for details.
- Voice conversations may be recorded depending on your administrator's configuration. Recordings are accessible after the conversation ends.

### Multi-channel Work
You may receive conversations across multiple channels simultaneously — chat, voice, WhatsApp, and others. Each active channel appears in the **Active Channels Pane** on the right panel. See [How to Handle Multi-channel Chats](../../How-to_Guides/Agent/Accept-a-Conversation.md) for guidance.

### Preferences
- **Language:** Change the interface language at any time in your **Profile Settings**.
- **Raise Hand:** If you need supervisor help urgently, use the **Raise Hand** icon to alert them immediately.

## Step 6: Logging Out
When your shift ends, always log out properly to release your status in the queue.
1. Click your **Profile** icon in the top toolbar.
2. Select **Logout**.
3. Confirm when prompted.

Closing the browser tab without logging out will leave your status active until the session times out.
