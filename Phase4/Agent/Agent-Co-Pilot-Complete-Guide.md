# EF Agent Co-Pilot: Complete Agent Guide

EF Agent Co-Pilot is your AI-powered partner inside the EFCX Agent Desktop. It helps you respond to customers faster by providing real-time suggestions, knowledge retrieval, and automated summaries.

## How It Works
Co-Pilot monitors your active conversations (Chat or Voice Transcripts) and uses AI to:
1. **Understand Intent**: Identifies what the customer is asking for.
2. **Retrieve Knowledge**: Searches the company's approved documents and web pages.
3. **Draft Replies**: Suggests professional responses for you to review and send.

## Key Features

### 1. Smart Assistance
As the conversation progresses, the Co-Pilot panel on the right will update with:
- **Suggested Replies**: Click to insert into your composer.
- **Knowledge Links**: Click to view relevant articles or PDF snippets.
- **Next Steps**: Suggestions on what to ask the customer next.

### 2. LLM Drafted Replies
For complex questions, use the **"Generate with Co-Pilot"** button. The AI will read the conversation history and draft a full, context-aware response based on the Knowledge Base.

### 3. Agentic RAG (Grounded Answers)
Co-Pilot doesn't guess. It uses **Retrieval-Augmented Generation**, meaning every answer is grounded in your company's uploaded PDFs and URLs. You will often see a **"Source: ..."** link so you can verify the information yourself.

### 4. Automated Summaries
Co-Pilot saves you time by drafting summaries at three points:
- **Bot-to-Human Handover**: See what the customer discussed with the bot before you joined.
- **Agent Transfer**: See a summary of what the previous agent discussed.
- **Conversation Close**: A final summary is saved for reporting and QA.

### 5. Post-Conversation Sentiment
After the interaction ends, Co-Pilot analyzes the tone and marks it as **Positive, Neutral, or Negative**. This helps the team track customer satisfaction trends.

## Agent Control
**You are always in control.** Co-Pilot provides suggestions, but you choose whether to use, edit, or ignore them. The AI is there to augment your skills, not replace your judgment.
